## The platform behind the platforms

[Kevel](https://www.kevel.com/) builds the infrastructure behind retail media. Its APIs let
retailers and marketplaces run their own advertising businesses - the
approach that built Amazon's ad business, made available to everyone
else. Retailers like The Home Depot, Chewy and Dollar General run
their media networks on it.

The platform grew up on AWS. After more than a decade in production it
ran deep: ad decisioning that answers in milliseconds, the data
pipelines feeding it, and the accumulated design decisions of a system
that has been quietly serving ads since 2010.

## One announcement, one condition

In June 2026, [dunnhumby](https://www.dunnhumby.com/) - the data science business behind Tesco's
Clubcard - announced the dunnhumby network alliance: a single route
for brands to run campaigns across Tesco, B&Q, John Lewis and
Waitrose, with more retailers expected to follow. Kevel is the
technology partner behind it.

UK retail media is forecast to reach £8.6bn by 2030, and the alliance
is one of the most serious attempts yet to organise it. The pilots had
a launch window: summer 2026.

Underneath the announcement sat a practical condition. dunnhumby's
estate runs on Google Cloud, and the alliance's decisioning needed to
sit next to the data it depends on. Kevel's platform ran on AWS.

## A second home, not a migration

Ologist engineers worked alongside Kevel's team to replicate the
estate onto GCP. Not a lift-and-shift, and not a rewrite. A second
home. The AWS platform kept serving customers throughout, while the
GCP estate came up alongside it, service by service, until the two
matched.

## Launched on time

The alliance pilots went live in summer 2026, running campaigns across

## The work behind the work

Most platforms are shaped by where they started. The useful work is
making sure that isn't where they have to stay. Kevel's estate can now
follow its market - new partners, new regions, new clouds - without
being rebuilt each time.

That's what we mean by Digital Permaculture. Systems designed to grow
and adapt alongside the organisations using them, rather than being
replaced every few years.
